# Space Remote Sensing Techniques

## Overview
Space remote sensing technologies are used to analyze celestial bodies and their composition from distance, similar to how we analyze archaeological sites from above.

## Key Technologies

### Spectroscopy
- **Visible and Near-Infrared Spectroscopy**
  - Analyzes light reflection at specific wavelengths
  - Identifies mineral compositions based on spectral signatures
  - Used for asteroid composition analysis

- **Mid-Infrared Spectroscopy**
  - Detects thermal radiation from celestial bodies
  - Helps identify silicate minerals and surface materials

### Radar Observations
- **Radar Altimetry and Imaging**
  - Bounces radio waves off celestial bodies
  - Determines shape, size, and surface roughness
  - Provides 3D mapping capabilities

- **Doppler Spectroscopy**
  - Analyzes frequency shifts in reflected signals
  - Detects density and composition clues
  - Measures rotational characteristics

### Advanced Imaging Systems
- **High-Resolution Optical Imaging**
  - Multi-spectral and hyper-spectral cameras
  - Thermal imaging systems
  - LiDAR and laser altimetry
